# Gate Grammar

The gate grammar is the public language for the load-bearing steps of a manifold-constrained program.

## Minimal Gate Roles

### Scope gate
Confirms that the admissible class and legal moves are explicit.

### Core gate
Confirms that a projected or protected core carries the needed stability, coercivity, or invariant structure.

### Transport gate
Confirms that local control propagates under the stated continuation or routing law.

### Restart gate
Confirms that re-entry, normalization, surgery, or restart is explicit and non-pathological.

### Compactness / persistence gate
Confirms that near-failure sequences or iterates cannot drift into uncontrolled behavior without being captured.

### Rigidity / exclusion gate
Confirms that extracted bad limits or degenerate alternatives are excluded.

### Bridge gate
Confirms that the constrained object identifies with the intended external object.

### Coherence / remainder gate
Confirms that all residual terms are tracked and that no hidden slack is being smuggled in.

### Final margin gate
Confirms that the endpoint closes with nonzero room rather than at a degenerate knife-edge.

## Why Gates Matter

Gates are not branding for lemmas.
They mark the distinct causal jobs a proof or architecture must perform.

A good gate grammar makes it possible to ask:

- which step is actually load-bearing,
- which failure mode blocks closure,
- what must be checked to rerun the argument,
- what would count as a real counterexample to the program.
