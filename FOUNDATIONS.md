# Foundations

This document states the philosophical and methodological foundations of the manifold-constrained core without turning the repository into a literary archive.

It exists for one reason:

- to explain the stable conceptual source of the method's architecture

## 1. Source and restraint

The method behind manifold-constrained work originates from a literary and philosophical development centered on the Moral Tensor Dialectic.

That philosophical archive is wider than this repository and contains:

- stable conceptual vocabulary
- rhetorical or symbolic gesture
- mythic and literary language

This repository adopts only the de-archival layer:

- tensor versus scalar reasoning
- curvature as remainder or local-to-global obstruction
- labor as constructive realization under resistance
- freedom as lawful motion inside a constrained space

The archive matters here as genealogy, not as certification.

## 2. The foundational contrast: scalar vs tensor

The parent contrast is simple.

### 2.1 Scalar method

A scalar method tries to close a system by one axis, one score, or one projection. It assumes that:

- the legal state space is already obvious
- the uncontrolled complement can be ignored
- identification of the endpoint is immediate from one internal representation

This is attractive because it is simple. It is also how methods overclaim.

### 2.2 Tensor method

A tensor method treats the object as multi-axis and transformation-sensitive. It assumes that:

- legality must be defined, not presumed
- coupled roles must remain distinguishable
- continuation, remainder, and identification can fail independently
- local closure does not imply global closure

Manifold-constrained core belongs to this second family.

## 3. Curvature and why the method is needed

In the source philosophy, `curvature` names what a flat formalism leaves out.

Here that translates into a concrete methodological warning:

- the constrained object can be lawful
- the remainder can be tracked
- the continuation can be legal
- and the method can still fail, because the bridge to the intended endpoint has not survived descent

That is why this repository does not treat restart, remainder, and bridge as optional decorations. They are distinct roles because failure modes are distinct.

If one wants a single sentence:

> The method exists because projection, remainder, and identification do not collapse to one operation.

## 4. Labor as constructive realization

In the source archive, `labor` means more than effort. It means structure carried through resistance until it becomes real.

In manifold-constrained core, that becomes:

- explicit proof work
- controlled transport
- restart and re-entry rules
- witness carrying
- honest accounting for what continuation costs

The method is therefore anti-magical. It does not treat closure as a declarative act. It treats closure as something that must be carried step by step through legal states.

## 5. Freedom as lawful motion

The archive's stable work about freedom is that freedom is not unconstrained wandering. It is lawful motion that preserves what matters.

In this repository, that means:

- an admissible class is not a weakness but a legal domain
- protected transport is the only kind of motion that counts
- restart rules preserve lawfulness when direct continuation fails

This is why manifold-constrained work explicitly names admissible sectors, bridge conditions, and margins. They tell us what motion is legal and when the method has actually arrived.

## 6. The architectural consequence

The parent philosophical vocabulary yields the core architecture:

1. define an admissible class
2. isolate a constrained or protected core
3. control continuation or transport
4. make restart and re-entry explicit
5. track the remainder as a first-class object
6. prove an identification bridge to the intended target
7. certify closure by a nondegenerate margin

This is the method in stripped form.

Its point is not ornament. Its point is to prevent the recurring false move:

> "Because I preserved one internal object, I therefore identified the target."

No. The bridge must still be proved.

## 7. What this repository inherits

From the broader philosophical development, this repository inherits:

- suspicion of flat overcompression
- insistence on lawful remainder
- insistence that identification can fail after local closure
- preference for architecture over slogan
- preference for explicit failure surfaces over implicit optimism

That inheritance is real and useful.

## 8. What this repository does not inherit

This repository does **not** inherit:

- mythic figures as technical evidence
- geopolitical interpretation as proof
- claims of adoption or influence without direct public record
- priority rhetoric as a substitute for method
- symbolic closure as a replacement for margin-certified closure

Those belong, if anywhere, in archive or genealogy material, not in the core method statement.

## 9. Claim levels

To keep the method public and reusable without drift, four claim levels should remain distinct:

### 9.1 Ontology

Why the method exists at all. This level can describe tensor/scalar contrast, curvature, or the need for remainder.

### 9.2 Architecture

How the method is organized: admissible class, protected core, restart logic, remainder discipline, bridge, margin.

### 9.3 Formalization

Named definitions, lemmas, and proof surfaces inside application repos.

### 9.4 Certification

Concrete closure artifacts, reruns, or theorem packages that establish exactly what has and has not been proved.

Confusing these levels is how a program loses credibility.

## 10. Why the method is general

The method is reusable across domains because the same structural problem recurs:

- local legality does not guarantee global identification
- continuation can break before the target is reached
- the complement can remain lawful but uninformative
- final closure requires a bridge and a margin

Mathematics, AI, control, geometry, and other systems all contain versions of this pattern.

The generality claim is therefore architectural, not mystical.

## 11. Final foundation statement

Manifold-constrained core rests on a simple conviction:

> A serious method must say what states are legal, what continuation is allowed, what remainder survives, how the target is identified, and what certifies final closure.

Everything in this repository is downstream of that demand.
