# Terminology Lock

This page fixes the core phrases publicly so they are not left to drift.

## Manifold-Constrained

A method is manifold-constrained when it explicitly restricts legal states, operators, or trajectories to an admissible constrained sector and tracks the complement rather than ignoring it.

## Canonical Lane

Canonical Lane is the theorem-library branch of the manifold-constrained program.
It is a family of public local-to-global theorem packages built as routed proof surfaces.

## Local-to-global gate architecture

A local-to-global gate architecture is a public proof or system architecture that decomposes closure into named causal roles rather than leaving the dependency structure implicit.

## Restart logic

Restart logic is the explicit rule for normalization, re-entry, reset, or surgery when continuation on the current representation is no longer directly legal or stable.

## Remainder discipline

Remainder discipline is the rule that the uncontrolled or unresolved complement must be tracked as a first-class object rather than silently discarded.

## Bridge / identification

A bridge or identification statement explains how the internal constrained object is recognized as the intended external object.

## Final margin

A final margin is the nondegenerate closure quantity that certifies the program does not only close at a singular knife-edge.
