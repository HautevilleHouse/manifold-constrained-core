# Comparison With Generic Architectures

This page states what the manifold-constrained architecture is not.

## Not Generic Geometry Branding

Using words like `manifold`, `geometry`, or `constraint` is not enough.
The method requires explicit admissible scope, explicit complement tracking, and explicit restart or re-entry logic.

## Not Generic Optimization

Ordinary optimization language does not by itself expose:

- admissible-class legality,
- restart discipline,
- explicit remainder bookkeeping,
- endpoint identification.

## Not Just MoE Or Routing

Expert routing or specialization can be part of a manifold-constrained system, but the parent method is broader.
It is about the architecture of constrained causal closure, not only expert partitioning.

## Not Mere Proof Packaging

The theorem-library repos use the method, but the method is not only a documentation format.
Its claim is architectural: it names the recurrent causal jobs required for closure across different domains.

## Positive Difference

The manifold-constrained method is distinguished by the combination of:

- admissible-class definition,
- projected core,
- local-to-global transport law,
- restart logic,
- remainder discipline,
- bridge or identification,
- final nondegenerate closure condition.
